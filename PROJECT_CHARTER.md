# PerkCommons Project Charter

**Status:** Founding draft for public adoption  
**Version:** 1.0

## Mission

Make legitimate perks, credits, grants, discounts, funding, and support programs
easier for everyone to discover, verify, and access.

## Public promise

- The ordinary public index remains free to browse.
- Individuals, communities, and companies contribute under the same rules.
- Payment never purchases verification, factual approval, or competitor removal.
- Sources, status, material eligibility, and review dates are visible.
- Published factual data remains openly reusable.
- PerkCommons collects as little personal data as reasonably possible.

## Scope

PerkCommons may index software, cloud, and AI credits; startup programs; grants;
fellowships; funding; student and nonprofit benefits; developer programs;
accelerators; and concrete business discounts.

PerkCommons will not knowingly publish fabricated offers, referral spam,
unverifiable coupon codes, deceptive financial schemes, expired offers presented
as active, malware, phishing links, unlawful services, or content requiring
unnecessary personal information.

## Verification standard

PerkCommons verifies evidence, not outcomes. A record may be published when a
moderator identifies a credible source, understands material eligibility, and
confirms that the destination appears legitimate. Publication does not guarantee
acceptance, availability, value, suitability, endorsement, or future validity.

Preferred wording is **“Source checked on [date]”**, not an unqualified
“verified” badge.

## Sponsorship

Ordinary inclusion is free. Sponsored visibility is clearly labeled and
separated from organic results. Sponsors receive no different evidence standard,
cannot suppress competitors, and cannot dictate editorial conclusions. A
reviewer with a financial conflict must recuse themselves.

## Governance

PerkCommons begins as a founder-led project and should evolve toward documented
shared stewardship. Routine listing decisions may be made by one authorized
moderator. Disputed removals, appeals, and sponsor-related decisions require an
independent second reviewer. Material changes to free access, data licensing,
editorial independence, or governance should be proposed publicly with written
rationale.

## Licensing and portability

- Software: Apache License 2.0
- Factual listing data: CC0 1.0
- Original documentation: CC BY 4.0 unless marked otherwise

Portable formats, public version history, and documented schemas protect the
project even if its website or maintainers change.

## Amendments

Maintainers may approve minor wording and operational clarifications. Material
changes to the mission, open data, free access, sponsorship independence, or
community rights require a public proposal and reasonable comment period.
