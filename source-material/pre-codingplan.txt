The pre-coding plan for PerkCommons
Stage 1: Freeze the concept

Write a one-page project charter. Do not start branding, registering entities, or buying ten domains until this is settled.

Working definition

PerkCommons is a community-maintained public index of legitimate perks, credits, grants, discounts, funding opportunities, and support programs.

Core principles
Access to the ordinary index is free.
Payment never determines whether a legitimate listing is included.
Sponsored positions are clearly labelled.
Listings link to official or otherwise verifiable sources.
Community submissions are moderated before publication.
The published dataset is openly reusable.
No company can buy a positive verification status.
Expired and misleading listings are removed or archived.
Contributor privacy is protected.
Major moderation decisions can be appealed.

These principles should eventually appear publicly as a PerkCommons Charter.

Define what counts as a “perk”

Create an inclusion rule now. A listing should offer a concrete opportunity such as:

software or cloud credits;
discounts;
free plans or extended trials;
startup programs;
grants and funding;
fellowships;
accelerator or incubator support;
student, nonprofit, research or open-source benefits;
educational resources with meaningful financial value;
competitions and hackathons with genuine awards.

Exclude:

ordinary affiliate offers pretending to be perks;
gambling, adult services, illegal products or deceptive financial schemes;
scraped coupon codes of uncertain origin;
unverifiable “contact us for pricing” promotions;
listings that require misleading users;
referral spam;
expired offers presented as active;
offers that violate sanctions or applicable law.

Deliverable: PROJECT_CHARTER.md

Stage 2: Decide what PerkCommons is not

This prevents the project from drifting into a coupon site, affiliate farm, or paid-placement directory.

PerkCommons should not claim:

that every offer is available to every visitor;
that a company endorses PerkCommons;
that “verified” means guaranteed;
that PerkCommons provides financial, legal or tax advice;
that sponsored listings are objectively the “best”;
that logos belong to PerkCommons;
that submitted data is automatically trustworthy.

Use language such as:

PerkCommons verifies that a listing has a credible source and appeared valid when reviewed. Eligibility, availability and terms remain controlled by the provider.

Stage 3: Perform name clearance properly

A normal Google search is not enough.

I found no obvious exact-match result for PerkCommons in a basic current web search, but that is not a legal clearance opinion. Trademark conflicts can involve similar spellings, sounds, meanings, unindexed applications and related services.

Search these name variations

Search at least:

PERKCOMMONS
PERK COMMONS
PERK COMMON
PERKS COMMONS
COMMON PERKS
PERKCOMM
PERKOMMONS
BENEFIT COMMONS
REWARD COMMONS

Search in:

EUIPO databases;
the Polish Patent Office, UPRP;
WIPO Global Brand Database;
company and organization registers;
domain records;
GitHub organizations;
major social platforms;
app stores;
package registries;
general web and business databases.

Pay particular attention to marks covering:

online directories;
downloadable or hosted software;
advertising and sponsored listings;
business information;
databases;
community platforms;
fundraising;
membership or benefit services.
Likely trademark classes

A trademark professional may consider classes such as:

Class 35: advertising, business directories, promotional information and possibly sponsored listings;
Class 42: hosted software, technology services or an online platform;
Class 9: downloadable software or datasets, but only if you genuinely plan to offer them;
Class 36: potentially fundraising or financial-information services, depending on what you actually provide;
Class 41: educational content or publications, if substantial.

Do not file broadly just because a class sounds useful. Every class costs money and an overly broad specification can create unnecessary problems.

Poland, EU or both?

A practical progression is:

Conduct clearance.
Establish who will own the mark.
File in Poland first if the project will initially be Poland-based and money is limited.
Consider an EU trade mark once European use and sponsorship become credible.

An EU trademark currently starts at €850 online for one class, with additional fees for further classes, and lasts ten years subject to renewal.

The 2026 EUIPO SME Fund offers qualifying EU-based SMEs reimbursement of up to 75% of eligible national or EU trademark fees, but you generally need to obtain the voucher before filing and pay before receiving reimbursement.

Important ownership decision

Do not file the trademark personally and casually promise to transfer it later.

First decide whether it will be owned by:

you personally;
a sole proprietorship or company;
a Polish foundation;
a Polish association;
another nonprofit entity.

The owner should ideally also control:

the primary domain;
GitHub organization;
logo rights;
social accounts;
sponsor contracts;
donation accounts.

Deliverable: a dated trademark-clearance report and an ownership decision.

Stage 5: Choose the legal shape

You do not necessarily need to create a formal organization before the first prototype. But you should choose the intended path before accepting donations, signing sponsorships or filing a trademark.

Option A: Start as an informal project

Best when:

there is one founder;
no significant money is moving;
you need to validate interest;
sponsors are not yet involved.

Limitations:

donations may legally be treated as personal income or business receipts;
contracts are signed personally;
the trademark and domains belong personally to you;
liability is less separated;
public trust may be lower.

Use this only for the validation period.

Option B: Sole proprietorship or company

Best when:

sponsorship revenue may appear early;
you need invoices;
you want faster administration;
the project may eventually hire contractors.

This is operationally simpler than building a nonprofit institution immediately, but it can make “community-owned” claims less convincing unless governance is transparent.

Option C: Polish association

Potentially appropriate when:

you have a genuine membership community;
contributors should elect leadership;
collective governance is important;
the organization will have multiple committed founders or members.

An association is generally more naturally democratic than a founder-controlled foundation.

Option D: Polish foundation

Potentially appropriate when:

the mission is public-benefit oriented;
assets are dedicated to the mission;
governance should be stable;
you expect grants, donations and institutional partners.

A foundation is not automatically the same as a U.S. charitable organization, and its tax, reporting and fundraising treatment needs Polish professional advice.

My recommendation

Use a two-step approach:

Validation period: operate carefully as a founder-led open-source project without taking major sponsorship commitments.

Formalization trigger: create the entity before any of these happens:

the first material sponsorship;
recurring donations become meaningful;
another person receives financial authority;
trademark filing;
paid contractors;
meaningful handling of personal data;
public claims that PerkCommons is a foundation or nonprofit.

For the long-term model you described, an association may align best with community participation. A foundation may align better with mission continuity and institutional fundraising. Discuss both with a Polish NGO lawyer and accountant before choosing.

Deliverable: a legal-structure memo with the choice, ownership plan, tax assumptions and formation trigger.

Partnership reviewer

Decision levels

Use a simple model:

disputed listings: two moderators;
sponsor-related listing decisions: moderator with no involvement in the sponsorship;
policy changes: public proposal and maintainer approval;
trademark, entity or major financial decisions: governing body approval;

Conflict-of-interest rule

A moderator must recuse themselves when they:

work for the listed company;
receive money or benefits from it;
submitted the listing;
have a personal dispute with it;
negotiated its sponsorship.
Appeals

Allow companies and contributors to appeal:

Initial moderation decision.
Review by a different moderator.
Final review by a small moderation panel.
Publicly recorded outcome, excluding private information.

Stage 7: Define the editorial standard

The value of PerkCommons will not be the number of listings. It will be the reliability of the listings.

Every published listing should contain
official program name;
provider name;
short factual description;
category;
benefit type;
estimated value, when objectively stated;
eligibility requirements;
geographic restrictions;
application process;
official destination URL;
source URL;
date submitted;
date last checked;
status;
submitter type;
sponsorship status;
change history;
moderator evidence note.
Status system

Use unambiguous terms:

Active: checked and currently available;
Unconfirmed: credible but not recently reconfirmed;
Waitlist: real, but applications are not currently open;
Limited: restricted by location, cohort or partner;
Expired: no longer available;
Removed: violated policy or could not be substantiated;
Disputed: subject to an active factual dispute.

Avoid a generic green “verified” badge without explaining what it means.

Better wording:

Source checked on 16 July 2026

That states exactly what you verified.

Evidence hierarchy

Prefer:

provider’s official program page;
provider’s official documentation;
provider representative using a verified company email;
official partner page;
credible independent reporting;
community reports with supporting evidence.

Do not approve listings based solely on:

a social media screenshot;
a referral link;
an unverified Discord message;
a copied aggregator description;
an AI-generated summary.
Review frequency

Suggested cadence:

time-sensitive funding opportunities: monthly;
startup and vendor programs: every 60–90 days;
stable student or nonprofit programs: every 180 days;
listings with an explicit closing date: automatic review near that date;
sponsored listings: same editorial review cycle as ordinary listings.

Deliverable: EDITORIAL_POLICY.md

Stage 8: Separate sponsorship from editorial inclusion

This is crucial for credibility.

The rule

Sponsors purchase visibility, never legitimacy, verification or inclusion.

Possible sponsor inventory
clearly labelled homepage sponsor;
category sponsor;
newsletter sponsor;
sponsored educational guide;
highlighted but clearly labelled listing;
organization profile page;
public supporter wall.
Never sell
positive moderation outcomes;
removal of competing listings;
false “verified” status;
altered ranking without disclosure;
guaranteed editorial language;
undisclosed affiliate placement;
access to contributor data.
Ranking policy

Organic results should use publicly described factors, for example:

relevance to the query;
current status;
completeness;
last review date;
geographic applicability;
alphabetical order.

Sponsored results should be placed in a distinct block labelled Sponsored, not quietly mixed into organic ranking.

Partnership documents

Prepare before contacting companies:

prohibited sponsor categories;

refund and cancellation terms;

Stage 9: Choose licenses intentionally

You have at least three different things to license.

1. Software

I would choose Apache License 2.0 for the code.

It permits broad reuse and includes an express patent grant from contributors, which can be valuable for a serious public project.

MIT is simpler and also broadly permissive, but Apache 2.0 gives slightly more structured protection for a growing contributor ecosystem.

2. The listing dataset

Choose between:

CC0

Best for maximum reuse. Others can copy, remix and commercialize the dataset without attribution obligations, to the fullest extent legally possible. Creative Commons describes CC0 as a way to opt out of copyright and database protection as far as the law allows.

CC BY 4.0

Requires attribution but permits commercial reuse and modification.

CC BY-SA 4.0

Requires attribution and generally requires adaptations to be shared under compatible terms.

My recommendation for the core factual dataset is CC0, because factual directories become most useful when anyone can reuse them without license anxiety. You can separately ask for voluntary attribution.

3. Original editorial content

Use CC BY 4.0 for guides, articles and documentation, unless there is a reason to require share-alike.

Contributor permission

Your submission terms need to say that contributors:

have the right to submit the material;
grant the necessary rights;
agree that factual contributions may be published under the dataset license;
understand that submissions may be edited or removed;
do not submit confidential information;
do not upload logos without authority.

A lightweight Developer Certificate of Origin may be enough for code. You probably do not need a heavy Contributor License Agreement on day one.

Stage 10: Plan trademark and logo use

You will index companies, so their names and logos require care.

Safer default

Use company names in plain text where necessary to identify the provider.

Only display logos when:

the provider supplies them and grants permission;
the provider has an accessible brand-assets policy permitting this use;
your lawyer confirms the intended nominative use is acceptable;
the logo is pulled from an authorized source under documented terms.

Do not imply sponsorship simply because a logo appears.

Include a statement such as:

Company names, program names and logos belong to their respective owners. Their appearance does not imply endorsement of or affiliation with PerkCommons unless explicitly stated.

Create a trademark complaint process. A rights holder should be able to request correction or removal through legal@perkcommons.org.

Your own mark

Prepare:

word mark: PERKCOMMONS;
logo mark later;
brand usage rules;
rules for community chapters;
rules for unofficial integrations;
a “not affiliated” requirement for forks.

Register the word mark first if funds are limited. Logos often change; the name should be more stable.

Stage 11: Prepare the privacy model

You will process personal data even with a static public index because submissions, moderation, emails and security logs can identify people.

The GDPR applies regardless of whether personal data is handled automatically or manually.

Minimize collection

For a normal submission, ask only for:

listing information;
source URL;
optional submitter name;
email address only when follow-up is necessary;
whether the submitter represents the provider;
agreement to submission terms.

Do not collect:

home addresses;
dates of birth;
identity documents;
unnecessary employment details;
broad analytics identifiers;
contributor IP addresses longer than operationally needed.

EU guidance requires personal data to be adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary.

Decide retention periods

Example starting policy:

rejected anonymous submissions: 30–90 days;
accepted submission metadata: one year unless needed for audit;
correspondence with companies: contract period plus legally required retention;
moderation evidence: as long as the listing remains active, then a limited archive;
security logs: 30–90 days;
unresolved abuse reports: until resolution plus an appeal period;
newsletter data: until unsubscribe, subject to legal recordkeeping.
Privacy documents

Prepare:

privacy notice;
cookie notice;
data retention schedule;
processor list;
data request procedure;
breach response plan;
record of processing activities;
international-transfer assessment for non-EEA services.

The privacy notice should explain identity, purposes, legal bases, retention, recipients and whether information is transferred outside the EU.

Have a way for people to exercise access, correction and deletion rights electronically.

A qualifying personal-data breach may have to be reported to the supervisory authority within 72 hours of awareness.

For every listing:

Do not try to build a magical “AI troll detector.” Use layered controls:
